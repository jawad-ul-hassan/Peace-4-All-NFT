How does it work?

We pay artists to create unique pieces of art with a peace theme. These pictures are sold on an NFT marketplace, and 90% of profit from the sales goes to the ICRC. Every NFT can be resold again, even at a higher price point. Each sale generates a further 10% from which we donate again 90%. Basically there is an income stream that will benefit the International Committee of the Red Cross in the long run when these NFTs sell.

Donations are made each week if more than €500 is reached; otherwise, donations are made monthly. We post the donation receipt right here on the website as proof. You can also sign up for our newsletter (link) if you want to be informed via email.

Our NFTs are "green", because they are on the Polygon blockchain. The new proof-of-stake consensus algorithm needs less than 0.01% of the energy of other conventional proof-of-work blockchains like Ethereum or Blockchain, which means very low CO2 emission. See here for details. This is the reason that other organizations like the WWF are also using Polygon for their NFTs, see here.

FAQ

  • Why buy Peace 4 All NFTs?
    • There are many reasons why people buy NFTs. Some like to collect them, others use them as an investment like shares at a stock market. However, our NFTs have the potential to make money for the ICRC again and again. With each resale of the artwork, more money will be donated. And you are free to use bought NFTs for any purpose you like. Resell them, use them as a profile picture, chat emotes, etc.
  • What is an NFT?
    • NFT stands for “non-fungible token.” This means it is something unique, like a unique trading card or a numbered entrance card. Money, in contrast to this, is fungible, because one $100 dollar note is the same as another $100 note and can be exchanged comparatively to each other; it has no intrinsic value. The Peace 4 All NFTs are unique artwork items. See for more details about NFTs.
  • Where are the NFTs saved?
    • The Peace 4 All NFTs are saved on the Polygon blockchain. The problem with Ethereum is that the transaction fees can be quite high, often up to $50 just to buy a single NFT. In comparison to this, transaction fees on the Polygon network are only cents. This means that we can donate much more money from each transaction. OpenSea fully supports Polygon. You can buy our NFTs with ETH as usual, and OpenSea automatically transfers it to the Polygon network. The vast majority of modern wallets which support Ethereum also support Polygon, so you will rapidly see your NFT in your wallet as well after buying it and in your OpenSea collection.
    • The metadata and images are saved on the (IPFS). This is a decentralized peer-to-peer network to save data. This means even when OpenSea would power off their servers someday, your NFT and image with metadata are still accessible, because IPFS is a decentralized service, designed to last forever.
  • Can I get a charity receipt when I buy these NFTs?
    • No. Strictly speaking, you are buying a piece of art that many would also consider an investment. We donate 90% of our sales for charity, but we are a normal company that makes money. If you want to make sure 100% of your contribution goes to the ICRC, then you can donate here directly instead: https://www.icrc.org/en
    • But, if you’d like, we can mention your name and contribution on our blog when we post our donation receipt.
  • Why not donating 100%?
    • To create compelling NFTs, hire artists, launch and maintain a website, do some marketing, run the project, and so on, we need money. Right now, we are paying for the entire project out of money from of our own pockets, but that can only last for so long. The 10% we keep is to make sure Peace 4 All NFT is viable in the long run.
  • How did Peace 4 All NFT start?
    • We’ve been interested in NFT art deals for quite some time, but we wanted to do something good at the same time. So, we decided to launch this project next to the normal NFTs we sell.
